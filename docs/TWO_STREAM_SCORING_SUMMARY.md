# Two-Stream Scoring Summary

Date: 2026-04-20
Scope: Summary of the scoring logic in the provided two-stream lending risk model

## Overview

The model produces three related scores:

1. `market_lending_score`
2. `liquidity_score`
3. `risk_taking_score`

The first score measures how favorable the external market looks. The second measures internal cash capacity. The third combines both into a final lending posture.

## 1. Market Score

The market score is built from external market data fetched from Saudi statistics APIs and SAMA POS data.

Formula:

```text
market_lending_score = clamp(50 + 50 * weighted_sum, 0, 100)
```

### Market indicators used

- Food and beverages POS sales, latest value
- Food and beverages POS sales growth percent
- Food and beverages POS transactions, latest value
- Food and beverages POS transactions growth percent
- Food inflation year over year
- Restaurants inflation year over year
- WPI national year over year
- GDP growth percent

### Direction of impact

- Higher POS sales and POS growth increase the market score
- Higher POS transactions and transaction growth increase the market score
- Higher GDP growth increases the market score
- Higher food inflation lowers the market score
- Higher restaurant inflation lowers the market score
- Higher WPI lowers the market score

### Weights

- `+0.20` food and beverages POS sales, latest
- `+0.20` food and beverages POS sales growth percent
- `+0.10` food and beverages POS transactions, latest
- `+0.10` food and beverages POS transactions growth percent
- `-0.15` food inflation year over year
- `-0.05` restaurants inflation year over year
- `-0.10` WPI national year over year
- `+0.10` GDP growth percent

### Normalization

- Latest POS values are scaled against their own history to approximately `-1` to `+1`
- Sales growth percent uses `tanh(growth / 10)`
- Transactions growth percent uses `tanh(growth / 10)`
- Food inflation uses `tanh(value / 10)`
- Restaurant inflation uses `tanh(value / 10)`
- WPI uses `tanh(value / 15)`
- GDP growth uses `tanh(value / 20)`

### Freshness adjustment

Each market indicator is multiplied by a freshness factor before weighting.

- Monthly data keeps full weight up to 3 months stale, then decays to `0` by 24 months
- Annual data keeps full weight up to 15 months stale, then decays to `0` by 42 months

This means stale indicators still exist in the model, but their influence is reduced.

## 2. Liquidity Score

The liquidity score is based on synthetic treasury and cash-flow history generated by the model.

Formula:

```text
liquidity_score = 100 * weighted_component_sum
```

### Liquidity indicators used

- Current cash percentile versus cash history
- Current cash z-score versus cash history
- Tail buffer versus 10th percentile cash
- Drawdown resilience from peak cash
- Stressed runway score

### Component weights

- `0.25` cash percentile
- `0.20` cash z-score
- `0.15` tail buffer versus p10
- `0.10` drawdown resilience
- `0.30` stressed runway

### Key liquidity logic

- Current cash is compared against historical cash balances
- A stressed monthly net outflow is estimated from recent activity
- Runway is calculated as current cash divided by stressed net outflow
- Better cash position and longer runway increase the liquidity score
- Larger drawdowns reduce the liquidity score

### Stressed runway mechanics

The recent 6-month window is used to estimate:

- average disbursements
- average repayments
- average interest income
- average operating expense
- average charge-offs

Then the model computes:

```text
stressed_inflows = 0.70 * (avg_repayments + avg_interest_income)
stressed_outflows = 0.90 * avg_disbursements + 1.10 * avg_opex + 1.25 * avg_charge_offs
stressed_runway_months = current_cash / max(stressed_outflows - stressed_inflows, 1)
```

## 3. Final Combined Score

The final score combines market opportunity with liquidity capacity.

Formula:

```text
risk_taking_score = 100 * (market_factor^0.40) * (liquidity_factor^0.60) * runway_cap
```

Where:

- `market_factor = clamp(market_lending_score / 100, 0.01, 0.99)`
- `liquidity_factor = clamp(liquidity_score / 100, 0.01, 0.99)`
- `runway_cap = clamp(stressed_runway_months / 6, 0, 1)`

### Interpretation

- Market conditions matter, but liquidity is weighted more heavily
- Even a strong market does not produce a high final score if liquidity is weak
- The runway cap acts as a hard constraint on risk-taking

## Risk Posture Labels

The final score maps to a posture label:

- Below `35`: `defensive`
- Below `65`: `balanced`
- `65` and above: `expansionary`

## Recommended New Loan Intensity

The model also derives a simple deployment recommendation:

```text
recommended_new_loan_intensity = clamp(risk_taking_score / 70, 0, 1.25)
```

## Important Caveat

The market score uses live external indicators when available, but the liquidity side in this code is not sourced from a real treasury system. It is generated synthetically from a simulation.
